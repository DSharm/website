I'm Damini, and I'm finishing up my MSc at the University of Chicago Harris School's Computational Analysis and Public Policy program.

I want to work at the intersection of tech and social justice. Over the last five years, I've held many titles - researcher, data scientist, data journalist - and worked in NGOs, local government, academia, and in news organizations to blend these two passions.

Professional Experience

  • The Marshall Project

    Data Fellow | June 2020 - Present | Remote

  • Cook County Assessor's Office

    Data Science Intern | January 2020 - June 2020 | Chicago, IL

    I built an application that develops statistical models for equitable and accurate residential property tax assessments based on inputs from users, including options to ingest data from SQL servers or open data portals and compare different estimation methods or model specifications. Furthermore, I developed a variety of visualizations and metrics to compare performance of different models including both statistical measures of uncertainty and industry-defined metrics of vertical and horizontal equity.

    See my contribution to the residential modeling sandbox application on GitLab

  • Associazione Antigone

    Open Society Foundation Rights and Governance Grantee | Summer 2019 | Rome, Italy

    I streamlined the data collection to visualization process for the NGO, which monitors conditions in over 200 Italian prisons, and built out an interactive dashboard that is published on Antigone’s website. I also used text analytics software to tag and extract insights from rich qualitative data collected from prison surveys.

  • Poverty Lab at UChicago Urban Labs

    Research Assistant | January - March 2019 | Chicago, IL

    I created and analyzed a rich census-tract level dataset from the American Community Survey to determine a tract sampling strategy for an upcoming Universal Basic Income study in Illinois. Additionally, I conducted qualitative research to draft legislation that ensured study participants retained their existing benefits.

  • Education Innovation Laboratory - Harvard University

    Research Fellow | 2018 - 2020 | Cambridge, MA

    I evaluated four school-wide randomized controlled trials designed to close the racial achievement gap by analyzing administrative and project implementation data, employing intent-to-treat and instrumental variable analysis. Additionally, I analyzed the impact of Department of Justice investigations into police departments on crime and policing by analyzing data from the FBI and over 100 individual police departments, constructing synthetic controls to create valid counterfactuals for investigated cities. Finally, I managed a field experiment designed to improve principal management practices in 90 public schools in North Carolina.

  • Cornerstone Research, Inc.

    Analyst | 2015 - 2016 | Menlo Park, CA

    As an analyst, I provided financial and economic analysis for commercial litigation matters. I detected potential insider trading patterns by analyzing datasets with over 10 million observations in SAS. Additionally, I used event study methodology and regression analysis to analyze patterns in clients’ share price.

In 2017, Cook County State’s Attorney Kim Foxx released an unprecedented amount of de-identified, case-level data a capturing snapshot of all cases that go through the State’s Attorney’s Office from arrest to sentencing. I created an interactive dashboard to allow viewers to explore the Intake, Dispositions, and Sentencing Datasets.

Exploring Inefficiencies in the Cook County State's Attorney’s Office

The Cook County State's Attorney’s Office has been criticized of having excessive lags in the process from arrest to sentencing. I cleaned and analyzed case-level data to better explore these potential inefficiencies through a series of visualizations.

R, ggplot | Code, Viz

COVID-19 Decarceration and Public Health

Our team used machine learning to quantify the benefits and potential costs of decarceration during COVID-19. My contribution included creating a database to store individual-level data from North Carolina Department of Public Safety and using it to predict the risk of recidivism. The goal of the exercise was to see whether such an exercise could be conducted ethically and without perpetuating racial bias.

SQLite, pandas, Jupyter, scikit-learn | Code

Content Analysis of American Prison Writing Archive Essays

I conducted a content analysis of essays from the American Prison Writing Archive. I built a scraper to create a dataset of the transcribed essays, and used counting, classification, structured topic modeling, and word embedding techniques to systematically explore this important and unique corpus.

Jupyter, pandas, BeautifulSoup, scikit-learn, nltk, gensim | Code, Paper

Mental Health Facilities in Chicago

In response to the closure of a large number of public mental health facilities in Chicago, I created a proof-of-concept web application powered by PostgreSQL identifying local facilities that users can access based on their needs, location and insurance plan.

Python, PostgreSQL, Bottle, HTML | Annotated Walkthrough

I created a toy financial portfolio application using React.js. The app tracks actual, real-time stock prices using API calls to IEX Cloud, and allows the user to build a financial portfolio by "buying" and "selling" shares. The app also stores the user's portfolio in the browser.
